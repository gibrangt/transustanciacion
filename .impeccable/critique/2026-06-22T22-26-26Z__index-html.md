## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: The site largely avoids generic AI output. The two-color dialectic (blue = Catholic, crimson = Evangelical) is semantically rigorous and carried consistently across all 17 interactive panels without exception. The CSS-only :target mechanic, the Duelo hero component, and the genuine theological density make the site feel authored. Two tells remain: numbered section markers (01–08) appear as scaffolded eyebrows on every section, and both tile grids (10 + 7 tiles) are structurally identical repeated units.

**Deterministic scan**: Exit code 2, but all 11 side-tab and design-system-color findings from the pre-polish run are now cleared. Remaining 4 findings are 3 confirmed false positives (single-font: detector only parsed one @import family name despite 3 being loaded; monotonous-spacing: full 6-step scale exists; overused-font: established identity) and 1 advisory (numbered-section-markers) which is documented established identity in DESIGN.md.

## Overall Impression

The structural substance is excellent — 17 content-dense panels with real primary-source quotes, a semantically rigorous two-color dialectic, and a principled CSS-only architecture that is accessible, SEO-indexable, and instant. The issues are editorial and IA-level, not visual: an inconsistent Videos section, a redundant labeling pattern that adds cognitive load, and — most critically for the target audience — 13 of 17 panels with missing source citations. The single biggest opportunity is completing the source links; without them, the primary audience (Catholics who want citable evidence) cannot fully use the site's main feature.

## What's Working

1. **The two-color dialectic is architecturally perfect.** Every component — tiles, panels, debate faces, hover states, response callouts — correctly signals debater identity through color without a single exception across 1,433 lines of HTML.
2. **The CSS-only :target panel mechanic is elegant and principled.** 17 detailed argument panels are accessible, SEO-indexable, keyboard-navigable, and load instantly — no JavaScript, no render-blocking, no blank flash on open.
3. **Content depth is exceptional.** Every complete panel contains real primary-source quotes, named Church Fathers, structured argument logic, and a faithfully-presented counterargument. This density distinguishes an apologetic resource from a blog post.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Section 7 (Videos) breaks the established heading pattern**
Every other section uses the `.section-head` wrapper (section number → kicker → `section-head__title`). Section 7 uses a bare `<h2>` and `<p class="kicker">` inside a `.container` div — a different weight, size, and indentation. On a site signaling scholarly rigor, structural inconsistency reads as sloppiness.
*Fix*: Wrap the Videos section heading in the standard `.section-head` pattern matching sections 1–6 and 8.
*Suggested command*: `/impeccable polish`

**[P1] Three-tier section labeling on every heading (number + kicker + title)**
`.section-head__num` ('02') and `.kicker` (mono uppercase label) both appear above every section title. Two meta-labels before the main heading adds extraneous cognitive load and is precisely the numbered-eyebrow absolute-ban pattern. The section number does the structural job; the kicker does the thematic job — both together on every section is redundant AI scaffolding.
*Fix*: Choose one per section by value. Use the kicker where thematic orientation matters ('Postura católica', 'Síntesis del debate'); drop the number there. Use the number only where sequence is informative (the three conflict items, the five conclusion points).
*Suggested command*: `/impeccable layout`

**[P2] Argument 10 panel is structurally empty**
Tile 10 ('Fragmentación') opens to a panel with only one introductory paragraph — no Church Father citations, no argument sections, no conclusion block, no counterargument response. A reader who works through all 10 arguments ends on a hollow entry.
*Fix*: Complete the content or add an interim state ('Contenido en desarrollo') so the user isn't silently presented with an incomplete panel.
*Suggested command*: `/impeccable harden`

**[P2] Third-party video titles in the Terceros group contradict the 'Fair' brand voice**
Titles like '¡Pacheco está desesperado! Refuto sus falsedades' and 'El FIN DE EDGAR PACHECO!!!' are displayed without curatorial framing. A skeptical visitor or Pacheco's audience sees the site as polemical rather than scholarly — directly undermining the stated 'Passionate · Learned · Fair' personality.
*Fix*: Add a brief curatorial note before the Terceros group disassociating the site from the tone of the titles. Alternatively, suppress raw YouTube titles and use curator-written descriptions.
*Suggested command*: `/impeccable clarify`

**[P2] Papal portrait images distort in the 3:4 book-cover ratio**
`.libro-card__portada` applies `aspect-ratio: 3/4` and `object-fit: cover` uniformly. Book covers work well. Papal portraits (roughly square or 4:5) are heavily cropped. The five Documentos cards are visually degraded.
*Fix*: Create a separate card variant for the Documentos subsection with `aspect-ratio: 1/1` or a horizontal card layout (photo left, title/author right) that handles portrait photos without distortion.
*Suggested command*: `/impeccable adapt`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer)**:
- 'Patrística' in the hero kicker requires prior knowledge; Section 2 explains it but not until after Jordan has already encountered the term.
- 10 argument tiles with equal visual weight and no 'start here' cue — no indication whether to read linearly or pick freely.
- The `:target` close mechanic (href="#cerrado") is invisible as a UI pattern; Jordan may not notice the close affordance the first time a panel opens.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent)**:
- When a `:target` panel opens, no ARIA live region announces the state change; screen readers won't automatically read the newly-visible content.
- Video group `<summary>` elements have no heading role — screen reader outline navigation skips 'Respuestas de Urbina' / 'Respuestas de Pacheco' / 'Análisis de terceros' as navigational landmarks.
- The mobile hamburger (checkbox+label) may be announced as a form checkbox rather than a menu toggle; should carry `aria-expanded`.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**:
- Terceros group lists 10 videos in a 1-column mobile layout with no pagination — very long scroll zone.
- The top-right panel X close button sits at the top of a panel that can be several screens tall on mobile, requiring a stretch outside the thumb zone.

**Marco (Project-Specific — Catholic Apologetics User)**:
- 13 of 17 argument/objection panels show 'Fuente por añadir' — the primary job-to-be-done (finding a citable source) fails in 76% of panels.
- Deep links (#arg-1, #obj-3) work but are never surfaced — no share button, no permalink affordance visible in the panel UI.
- No print or export path; copying clean argument content to a forum post strips all formatting context.
